How AI Can “Teach” Cars to Drive on Their Own

In order to drive on their own, autonomous cars constantly interpret images from their sensors and machine vision cameras, then use that information to make decisions about what to do next. They use AI to understand and anticipate the next movements of cars, pedestrians, and cyclists. This data helps them plan their moves in a split second, and decide what to do from moment to moment. Should the car stay in the current lane, or change lanes? Should it pass the car in front of them, or stay where it is? When should the car brake or accelerate? In order to make cars fully autonomous, Tesla has to collect the right data to train the algorithms and feed their AIs. More training data will inevitably lead to better performance – and this is where Tesla excels.
